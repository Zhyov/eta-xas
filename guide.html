Alphabet & Phonology

Alphabet:
The e'ta xas alphabet has 46 letters being those 37 pulmonic consonants, 8 vowels and 1 non-pulmonic consonant sound.

The alphabet is as follows:
a, a', p, b, B, V, e, e', s, s', S, z, z', Z, t, t', T, O, d, d', D, f, v, h, x, i, i', j, J, k, g, y, l, y', m, m', n, n', N, o, r, r', R, u, O' and -

e'ta xas does not have an uppercase version for its letters, thus, there is no need to capitalize the letter at the start because it may end up referring to another sound.

The format of syllables in e'ta xas is (C)V(C/KV) for the first syllable and CV(C/KV) for the rest. The symbols are C = Consonant, V = Vowel, K = Click and (◌) = Optional.

In e'ta xas, the first syllable is always stressed.

Grammar

Grammar Symbols:
  • ◌- = prefix
  • -◌ = suffix
  • ~◌ = suffix replacing the last letter of the word (only if the letter is a vocal)
  • (◌) = information, example, indicator or joiner
  • [◌] = definition
  • -◌- = literal translation
  • ~◌~ = translation
  • /◌/ = pronounciation
  • {◌} = word placeholder
  • \◌\ = text in e'ta xas
Grammar Rules:
  • i-{verb} = past tense
  • a-{verb} = present tense
  • u-{verb} = future tense
  • It is also grammatically correct to not add a grammatical time to verbs in e'ta xas.
  • {noun}-a = feminine singular
  • {noun}-o = masculine singular
  • {noun}-e = neutral singular
  • {noun}~i' = masculine/feminine plural
  • {noun}~e' = neutral plural
  • It is also grammatically correct to not add a grammatical number to nouns in e'ta xas.
  • ◌~◌ = letter connector (\luk~ne'\ /luk͡nə/ [five] connects the k and n in the word. Only consonant + consonant and vocal + vocal is allowed.)
  • ◌-◌ = glottal stop (\s'a-a\ /ʂaʔa/ [inside, interior] makes a stop in between the a's. Only vocal + vocal is allowed. This also counts as a vowel letter connector so it's grammatically correct.)
  • {noun} m'i' {noun/verb/adjective} = {noun} is {noun/adjective/verb} (\me' m'i' jun\ -I (separator) human / person- ~I am a person.~)
  • {noun} m'i' {verb} e' {object} = {noun} is {verb} {object} (\me' m'i' it'~r'un e' ni\ -I (separator) (past of) look (object) this- ~I looked at this.~)
  • {noun} {adjective} = adjective structure (\e'ta apon\ -language good- ~A good language.~)
  • {noun} {adjectiveA} pu {adjectiveB} = division of noun groups (\jun apon mut\ -people (good many)- ~A very good person.~ and \jun apon pu mut\ -(people good) many- ~Many good people.~)
  • o {message} = demanding (\o Si m'i' apon\ -(demand) you (separator) good- ~Be good!~)
  • {sentenceA} baS {sentenceB} = In the context of {sentenceA}, {sentenceB} or If {sentenceA}, then {sentenceB} (\tan me' baS le' ar' Zan Ren Du~aR\ -because me (context) we not can continue work- ~Because of me we can not continue to work.~ or ~We can not continue to work because of me~)
